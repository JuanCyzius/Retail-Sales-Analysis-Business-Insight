# Retail-Sales-Analysis-Business-Insights
End-to-end data analysis project exploring retail sales performance using SQL, Google Sheets and Power BI to generate actionable business insights
Project Overview

This project analyzes a retail dataset to identify key business insights, revenue trends, product performance, and customer purchasing behavior.
The objective of the analysis is to simulate the role of a Data Analyst working for a retail company, providing actionable insights and strategic recommendations to improve business performance.
The analysis combines SQL for data exploration and aggregation with Power BI for data visualization and dashboard creation.

Business Problem
The company wants to better understand:

- Revenue trends over time
- Which products generate the most sales and revenue
- Sales distribution across departments and sections
- Customer purchasing behavior
- Opportunities to improve revenue and reduce business risks

Tools & Technologies

- SQL (SQLite)
- Power BI
- Data Cleaning
- Data Analysis
- Data Visualization
- Business Intelligence

Key Metrics

Total Revenue: €39,854,875
Total Orders: 2,060,188

Average Order Value: €19.34
Average Revenue per Customer: €219.09

##Key Insights

1. Significant Decline in Monthly Revenue
Revenue shows a consistent downward trend throughout the year, decreasing by 82% from January to December.
This suggests potential issues such as:
declining demand
lack of promotional strategies
seasonal effects

2. High Revenue Concentration
Revenue is highly concentrated in a single department:
Department 4 generates 93% of total revenue
This creates a significant business risk because the company depends heavily on one department.

3. Sales Concentrated in Few Sections
Three sections dominate the majority of revenue:
Section 24
Section 123
Section 83

Together they generate over 90% of total sales.
This indicates limited diversification across the product catalog.

4. Strong Dependence on Organic Products
Top-selling products are mostly organic produce, such as:
Bananas
Organic Bananas
Organic Strawberries
Organic Spinach
Organic Avocados

This could represent either a competitive advantage, or a potential vulnerability if consumer preferences change.

5. Customer Behavior
The business has a large customer base but low-value purchases.
Key observations:

No individual customer contributes significantly to revenue
Orders tend to be small and frequent
This creates an opportunity to increase revenue through upselling and cross-selling strategies.

Business Recommendations:

Diversify Revenue Sources
Reduce dependency on a single department and a small number of sections by promoting other product categories.
Potential impact:
Lower business risk and improve revenue stability.
Expand Product Offering

Introduce new non-organic or complementary products to attract a wider customer base.

Potential impact:
Increase market reach and adapt to changing customer preferences.
Increase Average Order Value
Implement recommendation systems and bundle strategies to encourage customers to buy more items per order.

The Power BI dashboard includes:

Revenue trends over time
Top-performing products
Sales distribution by department and section
Customer purchasing behavior
Key business KPIs
